### TEMPORAL DRIFT GATE (Phase 178 — BiometricCredentialTTLGate)

Before any P0 or P1 advancement (dry_run=False authorization or token launch gate):

1. **Check credential age**: `GET /agent/biometric-credential-age` (Tool #127 Phase 178)
   - `biometric_credential_ttl_days` = 90.0 (config — FROZEN per BP-001 τ_half=90d)
   - `credential_age_days > biometric_credential_ttl_days` AND `renewal_enabled=False` → **HARD STOP: TTL EXPIRED, NO RENEWAL PATHWAY**
   - `credential_age_days > biometric_credential_ttl_days` AND `renewal_enabled=True, dry_run=True` → TTL renewal committed on-chain but not enforced live — treat as ADVISORY WARNING, not clearance
   - `credential_age_days < biometric_credential_ttl_days` → PASS; runway = `ttl_days - credential_age_days`

2. **Check temporal drift index**: `GET /agent/age-weight-analysis-status` (Phase 175 — AgeWeightedRatioPersistenceAgent)
   - `temporal_drift_index = raw_ratio - age_weighted_ratio` (positive = old sessions inflating ratio)
   - `> 0.10`: P1 non-stationarity detected — old sessions are pulling ratio above true steady-state; schedule 2+ new touchpad_corners captures BEFORE TTL renewal
   - `0.01–0.10`: mild drift — monitor; full 90-day TTL valid
   - `< 0.01`: biometrically stationary — full TTL valid; ratio stable across time

3. **Renewal pathway** (Phase 180 — BiometricRenewalEngine, Tool #129):
   - `renewal_enabled=False` (default) — operator must explicitly enable
   - `dry_run=True` (default) — commits on-chain but does NOT enforce live
   - Do NOT trigger renewal when `temporal_drift_index > 0.10` — stale sessions will seed the renewed commitment; new captures required first
   - Renewal resets TTL clock from `new_commit_hash` timestamp in `biometric_renewal_chain_log`

**Hard rule**: Never authorize `dry_run=False` (N≥100 live adjudications) when `credential_age_days > biometric_credential_ttl_days`. `separation_ok=True` in preflight is NECESSARY but NOT SUFFICIENT — biometric_ttl_ok must also pass. This rule mirrors the `all_pairs_above_1` requirement: meeting the aggregate gate does not clear individual component gates.
