# QorSense — Pronunciation Guide

**Brand**: QorSense
**Pronunciation**: **KOR-sense**
**IPA**: /ˈkɔːrsɛns/
**Stress pattern**: First syllable primary stress (KOR), second syllable unstressed (sense)
**Syllable count**: 2 (KOR · sense)
**Brand capitalization**: medial capital S — **QorSense** — emphasizing the Qor + Sense morpheme split, mirroring iPhone / GitHub / OpenSea / OpenAI / PayPal / DePIN brand-styling conventions

---

## Etymology

**Qor** + **Sense** = **QorSense**

- **Qor**: Intentional Q-without-U coined prefix. Semantically derived from "core" — the central, essential, foundational quality of a thing. Commercial precedent: **Qorvo** (NASDAQ: QRVO), the public semiconductor company (~$4.5B revenue 2023), uses identical "Qor" prefix pronounced "KOR" with the same etymological intent ("quality, originality, range, vision" per Qorvo's own brand materials). The Q-spelling is brand-distinctive and load-bearing — NOT a typo to be corrected to "Core".

- **Sense**: The English noun "sense" — fundamental perception, awareness, faculty of judgment. In the context of a verifiable-presence anti-cheat protocol: the core capacity to perceive truth (whether a human is genuinely present at a controller). Secondary semantic anchor (etymological): partial echo of "presence" via the "-ense" suffix, but PRIMARY anchor is "sense" the noun.

- **QorSense** = the **core sense** — the foundational perception layer that distinguishes verified human presence from synthetic/adversarial inputs. Phonetically: KOR-sense. Brand-uniquely: the Q ensures search-engine isolation from "core sense" (common English phrase, not a brand) and "coresense" (rare English compound, not a brand). Semantically: positions the protocol as the "core sensing" infrastructure for trustable physical-input attestation.

| Incorrect | Why incorrect |

| KEW-ressense | Treats Q as K-Y-OO; inconsistent with Qorvo precedent |
| kor-ESS-sense | Inserts unstressed 3rd syllable (vestige of Qoresence baseline, superseded 2026-05-17) |

| QUO-sense | Treats first syllable as "quo" (like "quota"); Q without U doesn't pronounce U |
| KORE-sense | Two distinct words with pause; QorSense is one fluid word |

## Brand Discipline

When introducing QorSense in spoken or written contexts:

1. **First mention**: spell + pronounce. *"QorSense — pronounced KOR-sense — is..."*
2. **Subsequent mentions**: use brand name only, no re-explanation.
3. **Capitalization**: ALWAYS medial capital S — **QorSense** — never "Qorsense" (sentence case) or "QORSENSE" (all caps). Like iPhone, GitHub, OpenAI, PayPal, DePIN.
4. **Lowercase slug** (URLs, file paths, module names): `qorsense` is correct (Unix/web convention).
5. **All-caps** (byte-literal domain tags, env vars): `QORSENSE` is correct (Layer B convention; never spreads to display).
6. **In presentations**: include audio sample (30s WAV) in slide deck appendix.
7. **In grant applications**: include footnote at first mention: *"QorSense (pronounced KOR-sense; etymology: 'core' + 'sense', spelled with intentional Q-prefix following the Qorvo NASDAQ:QRVO commercial precedent for coined Q-without-U technology brands, and medial capital S following iPhone/GitHub/OpenAI brand-styling convention)."*
8. **In FAQ / brand guidelines**: link this document.
9. **Internal comms**: gentle correction for mispronunciation or wrong capitalization (Qorsense / QORSENSE display). Brand recognition compounds — every consistent usage strengthens the trademark distinctiveness in future legal proceedings.

## International Considerations

For grant application materials reaching non-English-primary IoTeX foundation evaluators:

- **Romance language speakers** (Spanish, Italian, French): natural temptation to pronounce Q as KW. Footnote: "Q pronounced as K, following NASDAQ:QRVO Qorvo commercial precedent."
- **Mandarin Chinese speakers**: Q in Pinyin is "ch" (like "chip"). Recommended footnote: "K sound, not 'ch' sound."
- **Arabic speakers**: Qaf (ق) is a back-of-throat K — closer to canonical pronunciation than romance-language KW. No correction footnote needed.
- **German speakers**: Q always paired with U → "kv" sound. Footnote: "K only, no U."
- **Korean/Japanese speakers**: closer to canonical pronunciation when transliterated; minimal correction needed.

## Brand-Iteration Provenance (for trademark prosecution support)

QorSense brand evolved through three documented iterations on 2026-05-17 (single-session refinement):

Each iteration validated empirically (GitHub slot availability + 8-9 TLD NXDOMAIN check). Final form chosen for: phonetic efficiency + cleaner English first-read + medial-capital brand-styling convention + trademark distinctiveness + dual semantic anchor.
