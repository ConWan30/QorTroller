## Etymology

**Qor** + **Troller** = **QorTroller**

- **Qor**: Intentional Q-without-U coined prefix. Semantically derived from "core" — the central, essential, foundational quality of a thing. Commercial precedent: **Qorvo** (NASDAQ: QRVO), the public semiconductor company (~$4.5B revenue 2023), uses identical "Qor" prefix pronounced "KOR" with the same etymological intent ("quality, originality, range, vision" per Qorvo's own brand materials). The Q-spelling is brand-distinctive and load-bearing — NOT a typo to be corrected to "Core".

- **Troller**: From "Controller" — the primary subject of the protocol's verification architecture. Two reinforcing meanings:
  - **Surface read (literal)**: protocol cryptographically verifies physical gaming controllers (Sony DualSense Edge CFI-ZCP1 hardware per CLAUDE.md; future expansion to additional controller families)
  - **Deep read (agentic)**: the gamers themselves ARE the Core Controllers of their own gaming data — they hold the cryptographic credentials (PHGCredential / VHP), they grant per-category consent (CONSENT v1 primitive), they exercise GDPR Article 17 right-to-be-forgotten, they sign on-chain via `msg.sender`. This is the "Core Controllers of their gaming data" tagline made literal.

- **QorTroller** = the **Core Controller** layer — both the physical hardware verification surface AND the philosophical sovereignty framework where gamers retain ultimate agency over their gaming-data attestations.

## How NOT to Say It

These mispronunciations are explicitly NON-canonical and should be corrected:

| Incorrect | Why incorrect |
|---|---|
| kwo-TROLL-er | Treats Q as Italian/Latin (always KW); QorTroller follows Qorvo's silent-W pattern |
| KEW-troller | Treats Q as K-Y-OO; inconsistent with Qorvo precedent |
| QU-troller / QUOR-troller | Treats Q as part of "qu" digraph; Q-without-U doesn't pronounce U |
| kor-troh-LAIR | Stress on third syllable; incorrect — stress is on first (KOR) with secondary on second (TROLL) |
| kor-TROLL-eh | Italian-accented suffix; non-canonical English brand pronunciation |
| just "KORTroller" (running together) | Loses the morpheme separation Qor + Troller; pronounce all 3 syllables distinctly |

## Hostile-Read Mitigation Discipline

QorTroller phonetically contains "TROLL" — in gaming culture, "troll" carries the negative connotation of toxic player, harasser, intentional rule-breaker. Adversarial readers may extract "Troll" from "Troller" for mockery.

**Brand discipline to mitigate**:

1. **Always medial-cap in display**: `QorTroller` (Q-capital, T-capital, all else lowercase). This visually emphasizes Qor + Troller as a compound, NOT Q + orTroll or QorTrol + l + er.

2. **Etymology footnote at first mention** in formal documents:
   > *QorTroller — Qor (Qorvo NASDAQ:QRVO commercial precedent for Q-without-U coined technology brands) + Troller (from "Controller" — the physical gaming controllers the protocol verifies, AND the gamers who are the agentic Core Controllers of their own gaming data).*

3. **Tagline pairing**: at first mention, accompany with **"Core Controllers of their gaming data"** to immediately frame the semantic intent.

4. **Avoid in-house jokes that play on "Troll"**: discourage team Slack/Discord channels named `#qor-trollin` or jokes like "QorTrolled this commit lol" — the brand should be treated with dignity.

5. **Pre-empt in FAQ / brand-guidelines**:
   > **Q: "Doesn't 'QorTroller' contain 'troll'? Isn't that weird for an anti-cheat protocol?"**
   > A: QorTroller is *Qor + Troller* (from Controller), not *Qor + Troll + er*. "Troller" historically also means a fishing-boat trawler. The medial-cap QorTroller emphasizes the compound morphology. The brand is intentionally about empowering gamers (Core Controllers of their data), not about trolling — the verification proves you're real so trolls have nowhere to hide.

**Adversarial readings exist for every brand**: Apple ("rotten"), Amazon ("indifferent forest"), Tesla ("dead inventor"), Stripe ("just lines"), Patreon ("patron-izing"). QorTroller's hostile-read is an accepted cost of doing business in exchange for the brand's substantive depth (.com freedom + dual-meaning semantic + gamer-sovereignty positioning + IoTeX DePIN alignment).

## International Considerations

For grant application materials reaching non-English-primary IoTeX foundation evaluators:

- **Romance language speakers** (Spanish, Italian, French): natural temptation to pronounce Q as KW. Footnote: "Q pronounced as K, following NASDAQ:QRVO Qorvo commercial precedent."
- **Mandarin Chinese speakers**: Q in Pinyin is "ch" (like "chip"). Recommended footnote: "K sound, not 'ch' sound."
- **Arabic speakers**: Qaf (ق) is a back-of-throat K — closer to canonical pronunciation than romance-language KW. No correction footnote needed.
- **German speakers**: Q always paired with U → "kv" sound. Footnote: "K only, no U."
- **Korean/Japanese speakers**: closer to canonical pronunciation when transliterated; minimal correction needed.
